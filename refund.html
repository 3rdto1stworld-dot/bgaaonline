Refund & Resolution Policy

Transparent handling of issues in our curated engineering ecosystem

Critical Understanding: BGAA Engineering currently operates as a curator and directory service. We do not process transactions directly.

This policy explains how refunds and issues are handled in our unique ecosystem. We're building toward direct manufacturing services, but currently function as a bridge between creators and quality suppliers.

1.1 How It Actually Works

When you "purchase" through BGAA:

  • You're redirected to a supplier's WhatsApp
  • All communication happens directly with them
  • They set prices, terms, and shipping
  • They process payments and handle fulfillment
  • We're publicity partners, not retailers

Our Role: We vet suppliers for quality and reliability, then connect you directly. We're matchmakers for engineering resources.

2. Refund Responsibilities

1

Contact the Supplier First

All refund requests must go directly to the supplier you purchased from. They control inventory, pricing, and fulfillment.

2

If Supplier Doesn't Respond

Contact us with proof of purchase and communication attempts. We'll mediate as your connection point.

3

Our Mediation Process

We'll contact the supplier on your behalf and work toward resolution. This may include removing them from our directory if they're unresponsive.

2.1 What We Can't Do

  • Process refunds directly – We don't handle your money
  • Guarantee supplier actions – We curate, don't control
  • Store inventory – We don't have warehouses
  • Handle shipping issues – That's between you and the supplier

2.2 What We Can Do

  • Mediate disputes – We know these suppliers
  • Remove problematic suppliers – Quality control is ongoing
  • Provide documentation – Screenshots, chat logs help
  • Escalate appropriately – We'll help find solutions

3. Digital Resources Policy

Our CAD library and tutorials are free open-source resources. However:

3.1 CAD File Issues

If you encounter problems with our curated .STEP files:

  • Report technical issues – We'll investigate and fix if possible
  • Check original source – Some issues originate upstream
  • Use community forums – Other makers might have solutions
  • No "refunds" for free files – But we'll improve our curation

3.2 Tutorial Problems

Our tutorials are collected from open sources. If you find:

  • Outdated information – Engineering evolves rapidly
  • Broken links – External sources change
  • Incomplete instructions – Report for community improvement

Community Approach: We fix digital resource issues by improving our curation systems, not processing "returns" on free content.

4. Future Manufacturing Services

Under Construction: Our CNC/laser manufacturing system is in testing. This section outlines our planned approach when operational.

4.1 Planned Refund Categories

When our manufacturing service launches:

  • Design validation failures – Files we can't process
  • Machine errors – Our equipment failures
  • Material defects – Problems with our stock
  • Shipping damage – When we control logistics

4.2 Not Covered (Planned)

Even with direct control, some things won't be refundable:

  • Design flaws in your CAD files – Garbage in, garbage out
  • Material choice errors – You select, we fabricate
  • "Changed my mind" after production starts – Materials get committed
  • Cosmetic variations – Normal manufacturing tolerances

4.3 Our Engineering Philosophy

We're building systems with transparency:

  • Preview before cutting – Virtual simulations where possible
  • Clear tolerances – CNC/laser capabilities documented
  • Material samples – Physical references available
  • Iterative approach – Test cuts for complex projects

5. Problem Resolution Process

Our Step-by-Step Approach

1

Direct Contact First

Always contact the supplier directly with order details, photos, and clear descriptions.

2

Allow Reasonable Time

Suppliers need time to investigate. 2-3 business days is reasonable for initial response.

3

Contact Us with Evidence

If no resolution, contact us with:

  • Order details and dates
  • Supplier communication screenshots
  • Photos of issues (if physical products)
  • Clear explanation of desired outcome

4

Our Investigation

We'll review patterns with the supplier, check our vetting notes, and work toward fair solutions.

5.1 What Makes Resolution Successful

  • Clear communication – Specifics help everyone
  • Reasonable expectations – Engineering has tolerances
  • Documentation – Photos, chats, order numbers
  • Patience – Good solutions take time

6. Timing & Contact

6.1 Response Times

Realistic expectations:

  • Supplier response: 1-3 business days (they're small businesses)
  • Our mediation: 2-5 business days for investigation
  • Complex cases: Up to 10 business days for full resolution

6.2 Best Ways to Contact

For fastest resolution:

  • Supplier first: Use the WhatsApp we connected you with
  • For mediation: Email with "REFUND MEDIATION" in subject
  • Include everything: One complete email beats multiple messages

Engineering Mindset: We approach problems like engineers: diagnose, understand root causes, implement solutions, improve systems. We're building better processes as we grow.

Note: This policy evolves as we transition from curation to direct manufacturing. Check back as we build our CNC/laser systems and take more direct responsibility.
